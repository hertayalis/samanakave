Im a Beauty Editor With Acne, and This Skincare Device Clears Breakouts

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, May 30, 2024

I assumed that when I turned 25, my lifelong acne would just suddenly vanish. I associate blemishes with puberty and teen angst, not with an adult woman with a career and bills to pay. But, here we are. I am 29-years-old and I have at least one blemish on my face or chest at all times. These are not regular zits with whiteheads — they’re deep and big welts, mostly likely caused by my polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS). When they’re really bad (which is very often), I am so distraught that I head to a dermatologist’s office for a cortisol shot that quickly flattens out the welt. It costs roughly $100 per shot, which means I have spent hundreds, possibly thousands, of dollars on these in my lifetime. It’s expensive but there was really no alternative — until I found Riki Loves Riki’s Miracle LED Acne Patch. 

The LED Acne Patch kit comes with four components — a USB-C charger, adhering stickers in two sizes, a charging case, and the silicone patch that facilitates all the magic. Not only has it been transformative for my skin, but it’s also 20 percent off, no code needed.

If you’ve struggled with acne the way I have, you’re likely familiar with the proven benefits of LED light. A recent study found that blue light penetrates past the superficial layer of the skin where it reduces inflammation, which means a reduced blemish. It’s a claim that is backed up by years of previous research. My own experience with the acne treatment has convinced me of its efficacy, too. 

To apply, pick one of the two stickers depending on the size of the blemish/breakout area. Peel off the plastic on the side that says “down” and apply it to your skin. Then peel off the plastic on the side that says “up,” press the silicone on it, and apply the magnetic power button. Press it and let it do its thing for six minutes after which it automatically shuts off. 

As I said before, it works. It’s flattened-out welts that would previously have spent a month living on my face or that would have cost me $100 to get rid of. It’s kind of expensive so I recommend seizing this 20 percent off opportunity. Lastly, I will say that I tried a similar device that is half the price of Riki Loves Riki’s, but found it lackluster. It didn’t stick onto my skin, I didn’t see a marked improvement, and it capped out after 30 uses, whereas you can use Riki Loves Riki’s Miracle Patch forever. 

Head to Riki Loves Riki to buy the Miracle LED Acne Patch. It’s the most innovative and impressive skincare product I’ve used in a while.
