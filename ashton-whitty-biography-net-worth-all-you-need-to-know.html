Ashton Whitty – biography

Ashton Whitty was born on October 1, 1995 in Berkeley, California. Most details about her personal life and family background are sketchy, but her parents’ names are reported as Joel and Donna. Her parents were separated when she was very young. As a result, her mother remarried and had another daughter, Holli, who is Ashton’s half-sister.

From an early age, Ashton was known to have ideologies other than the general opinions she often expressed in her immediate family. At 13, she was already a #1 fan of Alex Jones, a radio host and conspiracy theorist whom she called her hero because his views helped shape her beliefs and ideology.

She was a student at Alhambra High School, due to her differing opinions, she was abused both physically and verbally by her fellow students. The constant boobies she endured in her high school didn’t deter her. it instead strengthened her belief in what she believed in and even made her popular, as other people felt.

The pretty blonde lady doesn’t bother as she is gifted in the art of creative writing and theatrical arts. She became an active member of her school’s theatrical production Once Upon a Time a Mattress . A music fanatic, she also didn’t hide her musical talents while playing drums for a punk rock band. She was a guest on Info Wars – a conspiracy theory and fake news website.

She later enrolled at Diablo Valley College before being offered an opportunity to teach at the University of California, Berkeley in the fall of 2017. Due to her conservative belief in a notoriously liberal campus, she led her contribution to the online publication called The Berkeley Patriot . Before joining The Berkeley College Republicans, she served as the campus correspondent for the Leadership Institute’s Campus Reform.

Her activism took a new turn in 2017 after she made several videos on YouTube that focused on free speech. She has also been very vocal on controversial issues such as gun laws, abortion and immigration, as well as President Trump’s travel ban, which has sparked heated arguments across cyberspace.
