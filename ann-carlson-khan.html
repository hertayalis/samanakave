Ann Carlson Khan Biography

An American Social worker Ann Carlson Khan is a dance enthusiast. She is also a choreographer and performance artist. Further, She is a president of the charity initiative, the Khan Foundation.

Ann Carlson Khan Early Life and Education

Ann was born on the 21st of October in the year 1954, in Park Ridge, Illinois, US. She holds American citizenship and comes from a white ethnic background. Also, her family background is not out in public.

Ann is a graduate of the University of Illinois, Champaign, Illinois, US. For her further studies, she attended the University of Utah, Utah, US and graduated magna cum laude with a BFA in Modern Dance in 1976. Furthermore, she learned her Dance from the University of Arizona in 1983.

Relationship Status

Ann is married to a famous billionaire Shahid Khan. He is a Pakistani American businessman who owns Flex-N-Gate, Jacksonville Jaguars of NFL, Fulham F.C of the English premier league, and lead investor of professional wrestling promotion All Elite Wrestling.

The couple met each other for the first time at the University of Illinois in 1967 and shared their vows on January 28, 1981, in Clark, Nevada, US. They have two amazing children together, son Tony Khan and their daughter Shanna Khan.

Professional Life and Career

Ann Carlson is professionally a dancer and has performed in Territory Dance Theater in Tucson, Arizona, US. Aside from that, she has worked with some of the famous personality including Susan Rethorst and Meredith Monk as well. However, in the 1990s, she left dancing and currently she is the President of the Khan Foundation.

Philanthropy

Ann and her husband, Shahid Khan are very active with charity programs and fundraisers. They started with Jacksonville Jaguars’ Foundation for the betterment of the community and now there are many more.

The Duo mostly provides children and family programs, fitness programs, military and veterans programs, women’s health issues, neighborhood revitalization. In addition to that, they also give away over 11k game tickets for every season. In the year 2016 only they donated $1 million USD for the establishment of the Baptist MD Anderson Cancer Center in Jacksonville, Florida, US. Moreover, For Breast cancer treatment, they raised $2.25 million USD.

To name some more they have supported the Jacksonville Veterans Resource and Reintegration Center, North Florida Boy Scouts, Ronald McDonald House of Jacksonville, City Year of Jacksonville, Edward Waters College, YMCA of Florida’s First Coast, the American Cancer Society’s Hope Lodge of Jacksonville, the Yeardley Lone/One Love Foundation, and the Jacksonville Humane Society.

The duo also focuses on education as they have donated to the University of Illinois’ Krannert Center of the Performing Arts, the College of Applied Health Sciences, the College of Business, and the library. Also, they have contributed to the funding of Five Khan Professorships as well as the Khan Annex.
