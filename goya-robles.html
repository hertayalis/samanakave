Goya Robles (born in 1990) is 32 years old actor and theatre artist who rose to significance in 2022 after playing the part of Joaquin in season 2 of Rob Hardy-directed series “Power Book III: Raising Kanan.”

Power Book III Raising Kanan Season 3 was released on August 14 on Starz and succeeded in gathering appreciation from the viewers. Robles has abundant experience working in shows. His first major project was “Get Shorty,” a comedy-packed crime drama that aired between 2017 and 2019.

Goya is the recipient of various awards, including Top Shorts Film Festival, Oxford Film Festival Award, and Festigious International Film Festival Award. He has worked in more than a dozen of short movies, including “Lollipop,” “Brut Force,” “Estilo Americano,” “Amy’s Gift,” “Narrow Streets,” and “Moth.” Currently, he is busy shooting for two movies, “Rattled!” and “The Greatest Beer Run Ever.”

Family Background of Goya Robles

In 1990, Goya Robles (age 32) took birth to his father and mother. In an interview, he described his childhood as tragic. He was three when his dad left him. Goya’s mother had to wash dishes at a local restaurant to earn bread for the family. Eventually, his mom remarried, but Robles grew to be an angry kid. He usually used to indulge in violence.

He suffered physical and sexual abuse from people, including his uncle. Later he was sent to a foster home, which made him feel safe. His school management categorized him in the “at risk” category at his school. At the age of 15, his elder sister passed away. She was only 17 and made Goya reckless.

He started bunking school classes, and by turning 16, he faced two felony counts and several other charges. His mother went bankrupt while paying the lawyer. In the end, Robles was sentenced to a year in a rehabilitation center. At the rehabilitation center, he saw officers beat up one of his friends so hard that the little boy started bleeding.

Writing Poetry

The situations he had to endure during his sentence motivated him to write poetry and raps. Later he got accepted to study music at the University of Bridgeport. During his first semester, he got robbed at gunpoint, and criminals broke his nose too. Finally, he decided to join the Latino fraternity, Lambda Sigma Upsilon, and it changed his life as a person.

Entering the Entertainment Industry

One day Goya saw a girl at a college party and fell in love with her. Later he got to know that she is a theatre artist and performs at Columbia University. He, too, decided to join an acting program and luckily got accepted at University in New York.

During his final semester, he got an opportunity to perform in a Broadway play called “Extremities.” His performance was so intense that viewers gave him a standing ovation. One of the viewers was Bradley Cooper. Brad met Goya outside the theatre and gave him compliments.

The following day when Robles went to class, his program director told him that Bradley Cooper’s manager wanted to meet him. From there, his career took off.

In 2010, he did a short movie named “Aftershock,” where he played the role of a street gang thug. Here we would like to mention that Goya is a versatile actor and can perform all kinds of characters.

After starting his career with a short film, Goya got the chance to perform in “The Civil War on Drugs” in 2011, which gave him some confidence to stay in the acting industry, and since that that he got featured in multiple films such as “Smile Now Cry Later” “A Gangster’s Word,” “A Winter Rose,” “Hustle vs. Heartache” and “Danger One.”

Currently, he has a lifetime membership of ASDC (Actors Studio Drama School). Besides working in movies and shows, he also teaches poetry at the prestigious Lee Strasberg Institute in West Hollywood, CA.
