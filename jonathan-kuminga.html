Jonathan Kuminga- Biography

Jonathan Kuminga is an 18-year-old Congolese professional basketball player who now plays for the well-known team Golden State Warriors in the National Basketball Association (NBA). In all professional matches, he played at the small forward and power forward positions, and he has won numerous competitive games and etched various titles on his name. Rather than attending university and completing his studies, he chose to play for the Golden State Warriors of the National Basketball Association. He had a strong interest in football since a young age and thus pursued his dreams.

Jonathan Kuminga- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Jonathan Kuminga was born in the city of Goma in the state of North Kivu in the eastern Democratic Republic of the Congo. Although no information about his parents or relatives could be found, his older brother Joel played for the University of Nevada. He was born in October of 2002 and celebrates his birthday on October 6th every year. Jonathan began playing football at the age of two and used to play in the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

He finished his freshman, sophomore, and other high school years at Huntington High School in West Virginia, United States of America, and he did not attend university for his graduate studies because he was selected in the NBA’s Golden State Warriors team with the seventh pick. Furthermore, he used to play football for his high school squad.

Jonathan Kuminga’s Professional Career

Jonathan had a strong interest in football from a young age, as evidenced by the fact that he began playing basketball at the age of two. Further, in 2016, he attended Huntington Prep School in West Virginia, where he played basketball for the school team; in his sophomore year, he attended Our Savior New American School in New York; and in 2019, he was regarded as the most valuable player (MVP) at the Slam Dunk after scoring 40 points, as well as winning the title of MaxPreps National Sophomore of the year.

At the conclusion of his junior season, he averaged 16.2 points, 5.5 rebounds, and 3.7 assists per game. The National Basketball Association selected him with the seventh overall pick in the year 2021 for the franchise The Golden State Warriors (NBA). Furthermore, by the end of his high school career, he was ranked third in his class by Rival.com and fourth by 247sports.com and ESPN.
